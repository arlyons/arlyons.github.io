Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

09/13/2015

When I think about my values, I think about my career and the trajectory it has taken over the years. Like a lot of people just out of college, I chose jobs based on what I thought would be the safest, or had the most potential for growth, but were ultimately very unfulfilling.

The breaking point was when I took a job cold-calling lured by the promise of huge sales commissions. I lasted two grueling weeks, surrounded by some of the worst, back-stabbing, greedy people I've ever encountered. I quit without another job lined up and felt really lost.

On an absolute whim, I walked into the wine shop down the street to see if they were hiring. It seemed like it would be fun to learn about wine and it didn't seem like an environment that would attract sociopaths. I was hired on the spot. The money was awful, it was incredibly fun and I made friends for life. I thrived there. I was promoted to manager after a few months and really enjoyed learning not only about wine, but how a small business was run.

I had ideas for changing some of the systems and procedures to make things run more efficiently and the company was small enough and the owners were receptive enough that my ideas were appreciated and implemented.

For the first time in my career I felt really competent and proud of my work. I fit in with the culture and really liked my coworkers. This was a real lesson for me that if you are guided by your values, things can really fall into place.
